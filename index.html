SCENE SIZE-UP

What we intended to happen was that you and your partner would arrive on scene and find an adult male seriously injured in a lawn mower rollover accident. A bystander is holding a towel to the man’s right lower leg, but this has not “stopped the bleed”.

PRIMARY SURVEY

You immediately direct your partner to provide manual c-spine stabilization while you apply a tourniquet to the patient’s right lower leg. Having confirmed that the tourniquet effectively managed this life-threating hemorrhage, you assess the patient’s level of consciousness, airway, breathing, and circulation. Based on this initial assessment, you provide BVM ventilation with supplemental oxygen. Since this treatment fails to raise the patient’s oxygen saturation to a 95% min., medical command directs you to provide a definitive airway, with continued oxygen therap. You perform sedated intubation, which successfully manages the patient’s airway/breathing, and cover the patient with blankets. Your goal at this point is to get this critical patient to a Level 1 or 2 trauma center as quickly and safely as possible.

SECONDARY SURVEY

Having identified that this patient is in shock and, generally, in critical condition, you direct your partner to take vitals while you perform a rapid head-to-toe assessment. Your secondary survey reveals non-serious bleeding, bruises, lacerations, etc. on the patient’s head, abdomen, chest, and extremities. After applying a cervical collar, you and your partner log roll the patient onto a long board. You retrieve and pack the patient’s amputated foot on ice, while your partner fully immobilizes him.

TRANSFER AND TRANSPORT

You and your partner transfer the patient onto a stretcher and into the ambulance. Your partner turns up the heat and drives to the nearest Level 2 trauma center, 25 min. away.

ONGOING ASSESSMENT AND MANAGEMENT

You contact the receiving facility, alerting them to the patient’s condition and your ETA. While en route, you establish two large bore IV’s; reassess the patient frequently; confirm that all interventions are still working properly, modifying as necessary; and dress as many wounds as time allows.
